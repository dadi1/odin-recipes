Feijodada

Description

This feijoada recipe is my version of a traditional Brazilian black bean stew that maintains the rich smoky, flavors famous in Brazil. Additional meats, including sausage, may be added if desired. This is excellent served over brown rice.

Ingridients

  • 1 tablespoon live oil
  • 1 and a half chopped onion, divided
  • half cup of green onions, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic chopped
  • 1(12 ounce)package dry black beans, soaked overnight
  • 2 smoked ham hocks
  • 8 ounces diced ham
  • half pound thickly sliced bacon, diced
  • 2 bay leves, crushed
  • one eight teaspoon ground coriander
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven. Add 3/4 of the chopped onion, green onions, and garlic; cook and stir until softned, about 4 minutes. Pour in soaked beans and fil with enough water to cover beans by 3 inches. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low, and siimmer uncovered for 2 hours, or until tender.
  2. While bens are cooking, place ham hocks in a smaller poit with 1/4 cup chopped onion. Cover with water and simmer until meat pulls of the bone easily, about 1 hour. Drain and add to beans.
  3. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 375 degrees F(190 celcuis)
  4. Place ham, bacon, and remaining onion in a baking dish. Bake until mixture is crispy, 15 minutes.
  5. Drain bacon and ham mixture and add to beans. Season with bayleaves, coriander, sailt and pepper. Simmer, uncovered, 20 minutes more.
  6. Stir in chopped cilantro and parsley just before serving
